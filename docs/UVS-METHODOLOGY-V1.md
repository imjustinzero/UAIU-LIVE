# UAIU Verified Standard (UVS) Methodology v1.0

## 1. Executive Summary
UVS is a real-time, IoT-backed, cryptographically auditable carbon-credit meta-standard designed to sit above existing registries. It closes trust, latency, and verifiability gaps for institutional procurement.

## 2. Problem With Existing Standards
Manual MRV delays, static registry states, and episodic audits create windows for double counting and confidence erosion.

## 3. UVS Architecture
- IoT verification layer with signed telemetry.
- Immutable audit chain with PQC-ready migration path.
- Registry cross-validation.
- Third-party verifier attestations.
- AI quality scoring and satellite cross-check.
- Anti-circumvention controls.

## 4. The 13 Certification Criteria
Each criterion contains: definition, measurement method, threshold, verification workflow, and breach action.
1) Quality score >=80 (AA+)
2) IoT trust >=80
3) MRV quality >=90
4) Signed third-party statement
5) Registry current/active
6) OFAC/UN/EU sanctions clean
7) Beneficial ownership disclosed
8) Open investigations = 0
9) Collusion flags = 0
10) Article 6 documented
11) Valid device signatures >=95%
12) Satellite-ground consistency
13) No active legal holds

## 5. Credit Type Methodologies
REDD+, biogas, renewable energy, blue carbon, cookstoves, and soil carbon pathways with project-specific coefficients and uncertainty bounds.

## 6. IoT Data Standards
Approved sensors, calibration cadence, signature protocol, anomaly thresholds, and satellite harmonization controls.

## 7. MRV Calculation Methodology
GHG Protocol-aligned formulas for additionality, leakage, permanence, and uncertainty quantification.

## 8. Verifier Requirements
Approved verifier criteria, field protocol, digital attestation controls, and independence requirements.

## 9. Governance
Standards committee structure, revisions, appeals, suspension/revocation policy.

## 10. International Alignment
Paris Article 6, CORSIA pathway, ICAO alignment, ISO 14064, and IPCC AR6 references.
