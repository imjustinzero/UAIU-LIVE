# Methodology Quality Index (MQI)

## What MQI is
MQI is UAIU.LIVE/X's cryptographically signed methodology quality score (0-100) for carbon verification methodologies. Each MQI snapshot is signed (HMAC), timestamped, and anchored to the UAIU audit chain.

## How MQI is calculated
MQI component weights:
- Peer Citation Score — 25%
- Standards Alignment — 20%
- Real-World Usage — 20%
- Outcome Tracking — 20%
- Verifier Reputation — 10%
- Academic References — 5%

### Component definitions
1. **Peer Citation Score (25%)**
   - Number and quality of partner references.
   - Self-citations excluded.
2. **Standards Alignment (20%)**
   - Alignment against ISO 14064-2/3, GHG Protocol, Verra VCS, Gold Standard, and future ISO/GHG joint standard.
3. **Real-World Usage (20%)**
   - Verified usage depth, recency, and diversity (geography + registries).
4. **Outcome Tracking (20%)**
   - Active/non-disputed status, UVS outcomes, fraud-free rate, IoT Trust performance.
5. **Verifier Reputation (10%)**
   - Reputation of practitioners applying the methodology.
6. **Academic References (5%)**
   - DOI and conference/paper references.

## MQI grades
- AAA, AA, A, BBB, BB, B, C

## How MQI affects credit pricing
Credits associated with stronger methodologies can command a premium when buyers prioritize integrity, durability, and reduced reputational risk.

## Price premium tracking
The platform tracks monthly premium spread between high-MQI (AA+) and lower-MQI methodologies, and displays comparative premium percentage on methodology detail views.

## How to improve your methodology MQI
- Increase external citations by reputable verifiers.
- Improve conformity with recognized standards.
- Drive consistent, recent, geographically diverse deployment.
- Improve real-world outcomes and dispute-free performance.
- Recruit high-reputation verifiers.
- Add peer-reviewed publication links and DOI references.
