# Digital Twin on UAIU.LIVE/X

## What the Digital Twin is and how it works
The Digital Twin is a fused, living state model for each project. It combines telemetry, satellite observations, weather, MRV/compliance, anomalies, and market context into one consistent object that can be queried in real-time.

## Data sources and fusion methodology
- IoT: device status + latest CO₂/methane/environment readings.
- Satellite: NDVI and vegetation-derived indicators.
- Open-Meteo: current weather and drought/fire pressure factors.
- MRV + Trust: quality, verifier activity, and compliance context.
- Market: latest listing price and demand proxies.
Fusion applies source-priority rules and fallbacks (e.g., estimated values when sensors are missing).

## Confidence score explained
Confidence is 0–100 and reflects data-source coverage and freshness. Projects below 60 are auto-flagged in intelligence events for operational follow-up.

## How predictions are generated
Prediction jobs run from fused history snapshots and use statistical baselines (moving averages + trend adjustments) with confidence intervals. Explanations are generated by Anthropic when configured.

## Accuracy tracking and model improvement
Predictions store eventual actuals and accuracy fields. Accuracy endpoints expose historical model performance so reliability can be tracked over time and models tuned.

## How verifiers use the pre-visit briefing
The Digital Twin surfaces trend changes, unresolved anomalies, device risk, and credit-vs-projection drift. Verifiers can generate concise pre-visit packets for field prioritization.

## Portfolio twin for institutional buyers
Portfolio Twin aggregates all project-level twins into one risk/compliance/forecast lens, including concentration risk and AI recommendations for diversification.
